# Journal Club - Quick Start Guide

## How to Add a New Post

### Step 1: Open the Template
Open `journal-club-post-template.html` in any text editor

### Step 2: Copy the Template
Copy the section under `<!-- JOURNAL CLUB POST TEMPLATE -->` (lines 9-50)

### Step 3: Edit journal-club.html
1. Open `journal-club.html`
2. Find the comment: `<!-- TO ADD A NEW POST: -->`
3. Paste your copied template **right after** line 43 (below the instruction comment)
4. **Most recent posts go first** - add new posts at the top

### Step 4: Fill In Your Content
Replace the [PLACEHOLDERS]:

- **[Post Title]** - Your perspective or takeaway (e.g., "High-Fat Diets Reshape the Gut Microbiome")
- **[Month Day, Year]** - Today's date (e.g., "January 14, 2026")
- **[Paper Title]** - Full title of the paper
- **[Authors]** - First author et al. (e.g., "Chassaing B et al.")
- **[Journal Name]** - Journal name in italics
- **[Year]** - Publication year
- **[PubMed or DOI link]** - Link to the paper
- **Key Findings** - 2-3 sentence summary
- **Why This Matters** - Clinical/research significance
- **My Thoughts** - Your analysis, critique, questions
- **Connection to My Research** (optional) - How it relates to your work

### Step 5: Commit and Push
```bash
cd hazleton-lab
git add journal-club.html
git commit -m "Add journal club post: [Post Title]"
git push
```

Your post will be live in about 1 minute!

## Tips for Great Posts

- **Be authentic** - Share your genuine thoughts and questions
- **Add context** - Explain why you're reading this paper
- **Make connections** - Link to your own research or other papers
- **Keep it focused** - 300-500 words is perfect
- **Add regularly** - Even 1-2 posts per month helps SEO and engagement

## Template Sections Explained

### Key Findings
Brief, objective summary of what the paper found. Keep scientific but accessible.

### Why This Matters
The "so what?" - clinical implications, research directions, broader significance.

### My Thoughts
Your personal take - agree/disagree? Questions raised? Methodological concerns? Exciting implications?

### Connection to My Research (Optional)
How this relates to your C. difficile, microbiome, or liver disease work. Great for showing your expertise!

Each post you add:
- Creates fresh, keyword-rich content (Google loves this!)
- Establishes you as an expert in your field
- Gives people more pages to find via search
- Provides shareable content for LinkedIn/Twitter

Aim for at least 1 post per month for best results.
