High-Fat Diets Reshape the Gut Microbiome in Unexpected Ways

Posted on January 14, 2026
Western Diet Induces Dysbiosis With Increased E. coli in CEABAC10 Mice, Alters Host Barrier Function Favouring AIEC Colonisation
Chassaing B, Koren O, et al., Gut, 2017
Read the paper

Key Findings

This study demonstrates that Western-style diets (high in fat and sugar) dramatically alter gut barrier function and promote colonization by adherent-invasive E. coli. The authors show that diet-induced changes occur rapidly and persist even after antibiotic treatment.

Why This Matters

Understanding how diet shapes the microbiome has direct clinical implications for managing inflammatory bowel disease and preventing opportunistic infections. The rapid time course (days to weeks) suggests dietary interventions could work faster than we previously thought.

My Thoughts

The most striking finding is how quickly barrier function degrades - within 2 weeks of dietary changes. This aligns with what we're seeing in our C. difficile work, where high-fat diets increase mortality rates. I wonder if the mechanism involves similar barrier disruption or if bile acid changes play a larger role.

Connection to My Research

This directly relates to our 2022 NPJ Biofilms & Microbiomes paper showing dietary fat promotes C. difficile mortality in mice. Both studies suggest that diet-induced dysbiosis creates a more permissive environment for pathogenic bacteria. It would be interesting to test whether protective dietary interventions could reverse susceptibility to C. difficile infection.
