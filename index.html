Research Overview

The Hazleton Lab investigates the complex relationships between nutrition, infection, and hepatic disease. Based at Children's Hospital Los Angeles, our work focuses on understanding how dietary factors influence disease pathogenesis and outcomes in pediatric gastroenterology and hepatology.

We collaborate closely with the Lozupone Lab at the University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus to leverage cutting-edge microbiome research methods.

Current Research Areas

  • Dietary influences on C. difficile infection
    Exploring how nutritional interventions and dietary patterns affect susceptibility to and outcomes of Clostridioides difficile infection in children. Recent work (Apgar et al., iScience 2026) shows that dietary fiber reduces mortality from secondary sepsis in a murine model of CDI.
  • Portal hypertension management
    Investigating novel approaches to managing portal hypertension in pediatric liver disease
  • Microbiome-liver disease interactions
    Understanding the gut-liver axis and how the microbiome influences liver disease progression and response to treatment

Recent News

New · April 2026

Dietary fiber reduces mortality from secondary sepsis in a murine model of Clostridioides difficile infection

Apgar M, Wall ES, Midura-Kiela M, Neff CP, Orlicky DJ, Lyon LM, Bernardazzi C, Martin CG, Gordon OM, Jimenez-Espinoza I, Flynn JK, Lozupone CA, Hazleton KZ
iScience. 2026;29(4):115258

Mice fed low-fiber diets showed elevated mortality from CDI, driven by translocation of intestinal pathobionts and secondary sepsis — ameliorated by fiber supplementation, suggesting a protective role for dietary fiber via intestinal barrier function.
